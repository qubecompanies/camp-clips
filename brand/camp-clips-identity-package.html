08

Voice · warm + plainspoken

Microcopy guidelines. The brand sounds like a calm friend who's done this before — never corporate, never cute, never apologetic.

Do
"Add some photos to get started."
Don't
"No photos detected in workspace."
Do
"That HEIC file didn't convert. You can try saving as a JPG instead."
Don't
"ERROR: heic2any conversion failed (code 0x42)."
Do
"Photos shuffled — hit Play to see the new order."
Don't
"Operation completed successfully."
Do
"Match music length needs songs first."
Don't
"Please add audio files to proceed with this option."
01

Use contractions.

"It's", "won't", "you'll". Anything else sounds like a help-desk script.

02

Be specific.

Not "An error occurred" — "That HEIC photo didn't convert." Tell people what happened and what to try.

03

Skip the apology.

No "Sorry!" or "Unfortunately…" — just say what went wrong and how to move forward.

09

Usage rules

Things to never do with the brand, however tempting.

Don't

Skew or rotate the mark.

The aperture geometry is exact. Never stretch, skew, rotate, or recolor outside the palette.

Don't

Use a gradient on the mark.

Solid colors only. The original three-blade design relies on flat color contrast.

Don't

Place the mark on busy photos.

Always use a solid amber, indigo, ink, or paper background. If you need it on a photo, put it in a colored chip first.

Do

Add clear space.

Always leave clear space around the mark equal to 25% of its width on every side.

Do

Lead with one color.

In any layout, one of amber/indigo dominates. Never split them 50/50 — pick a hero.

Do

Use italic Fraunces for accent.

The italic-on-the-tagline is a signature moment. "Slideshows Made Simple."
